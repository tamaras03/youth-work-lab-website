About the Project

A Journey of Digital Innovation and Youth Empowerment

The Project Vision

This 8-day Erasmus+ training course responds to the needs of especially the beginner youth workers for mutual learning, inspiring with positive practices and jointly planning new projects.

Through practical workshops, peer learning, and study visits, participants strengthen intercultural communication skills, improve inclusive approaches and learn how to better integrate new technologies in youth work.

The Youth Worker's Toolbox

Explore the non-formal education methods we practiced during the training:

Name Juggling

Building intense group focus and memorizing names quickly through physical coordination.

Blindfold Trust Walk

A deep exercise in vulnerability and non-verbal communication to build profound trust.

Agree/Disagree Barometer

Using physical movement to encourage critical thinking and respectful debate on complex topics.

Speed Dating Clock

Rapid, low-pressure networking to help participants find common ground and break the ice.

Team Target Ball

Improving teamwork and strategic planning while navigating group dynamics under pressure.

Active Energizers

Spiking energy levels and refocusing the group with fast-paced games like "Washing Machine."

Core Highlights

Study Visit to CED

The group visited the Center for Education and Development (CED) in Tearce. We explored how local organizations support youth through entrepreneurship and intercultural dialogue.

Intercultural Evenings

We celebrated diversity through two major evenings where participants from over 10 countries shared traditional dances, music, and food, fostering global connections.
